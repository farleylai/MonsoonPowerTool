Gradient Descent

28 January 2016

  • Negative gradient towards the minimum and positive one towards the maximum.

Find \(A\) and \(b\) such that \(x\) is the solution to the system of equations.

\(Ax-b=0\)

Let the \(F(x)\) be the squared difference and solve the linear least squares.

\(F(x)=|Ax-b|^2\)

The derivative \(\Delta F(x) = 2A^T(Ax-b)\) is the gradient.

Linear Least Square

Mathematically, linear least squares is the problem of approximately solving an overdetermined system of linear equations, where the best approximation is defined as that which minimizes the sum of squared differences between the data values and their corresponding modeled values.
