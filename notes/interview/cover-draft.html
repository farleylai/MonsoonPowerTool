Cover Letter

01 October 2015

Dear Sir/Madam,

Hope this letter finds you well. I would like to first take this opportunity to introduce myself as a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Iowa. My expected graduation date would be after August of 2016. I am currently looking for a 2016 summer internship with esteemed companies like yours.

I have published several papers on mobile sensing applications (MSAs) in IPSN, EMSOFT and CBMS. My recent research focuses on the performance and memory management of stream processing being the core of MSAs. We evaluate the performance on both desktop and the Android platform. My curriculum is comprised of courses such as distributed systems, design and analysis of algorithms, theory of computation, randomized algorithms, big data technology, pattern recognition, signal processing, image processing, formal methods, high performance computer architectures and more. In the pattern recognition class project, I manually implemented the MFCC-GMM based speaker identification in MATLAB and scored A+.

My prior work experience in the surveillance industry involves developing multi-channel video streaming software on both Linux-based network camera devices and Windows-based PCs. C, C++ and Java are primary work languages but adapting to new languages on demand is no problem. For example, I had been manually decompiling Moto 68K assembly into C for Magnetic Ink Character Recognition and writing an embedded web server in Perl for video conferencing products.

Instead of staying in the comfort zone I am already good at, I would like to take challenges and push myself to the limit that I am not sure where it can be. Alternating between various roles from camp host, theorem prover, software engineer to project leader diversifies my skills. I am not opting for one over another but in search of continuous improvement to make singular contributions.

I have enclosed my resume and would appreciate an opportunity to talk with you. Should you have any questions, please feel free to contact me. I look forward to hearing from you soon.

Cordially, Farley Lai
